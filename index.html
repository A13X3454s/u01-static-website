Our story

Now where could my pipe be?

- Jon Arbuckle (07/27/1978)

The story here is of a man, a plain man.
He is Jon, but he is more than that. We will get to this later, but first, let us just say he is Jon, a plain man, and then there is a cat, Garfield.

This is the nature of the world here. When we see the world, the politics, the future, satellites in space, and the people who put them there.

You could look at everything as a man and a cat.
Two beings, in harmony, and at war.

A comic strip about this man, Jon, and the cat, Garfield, it is about everything. This little comic is, oh, lo and behold, not so little anymore.

Jon Arbuckle, he sits here, legs crossed, comfortable in his home and he reads his newspaper. The news of the world perhaps.

Then he extends his fingers, lightly, delicately, he taps his fingers on an end table and he feels for something. What is it? It is something he needs, but it is not there.

Then he looks up, slightly cockeyed and he thinks... his newspaper in his lap now, and he thinks this: "Now where could my pipe be?"…
